Countless and Absurd

The name of this website, Countless and Absurd, is a reference to Hecataeus, reflecting my belief that logography is as necessary in post-modernity as it was in 6th century BC Ionian Greece.

I’m currently working as a researcher with the Median Group supported by a grant from the Berkeley Existential Risk Initiative. I also work in a part time capacity for Kalix Systems.

Software

  • Insights-based model of progress in AI: Subjective assessment + math. Implemented in Rust, compiled to WASM. (source).
  • Haskell script for extracting text and math from TeX source: Limited to the subset of LaTeX supported by pandoc (so no macro-magic), but TeX comes to us from the implausibly distant past (i.e., the late 1970s) and doesn’t have a parse-tree, so I’ll take what I can get.
  • Extension for Python’s markdown parser.
